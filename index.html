Fractal Dimension Analysis

Analysis Section

The fractal image of Bengaluru's urban structure reveals a complex, irregular growth pattern typical of rapidly expanding metropolitan areas, characterized by a high fractal dimension value (likely between 1.5-1.8). This indicates a mix of planned and organic development, with dense urban cores surrounded by fragmented suburban sprawl. The branching patterns suggest radial expansion along major transport corridors (e.g., Hosur Road, Bellary Road) interspersed with discontinuous growth in peripheral areas. The texture shows both fine-grained infill development and larger-scale commercial/residential projects, reflecting Bengaluru's transition from a nodal city to a dispersed metropolitan region. This fractal complexity highlights the need for integrated planning to address infrastructure gaps in rapidly urbanizing fringes while maintaining connectivity to the city center.
